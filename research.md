# Research

My research lies at the intersection of **signal processing**, **deep learning**, and **biomedical imaging**.
I develop methods that improve the quality and information content of medical images — primarily ultrasound
and MRI — to support automated diagnosis and clinical decision-making. A recurring theme across my work
is the design of computationally efficient methods that remain robust in real clinical conditions.

## Research Areas

**Super-Resolution & Denoising of Biomedical Images**: I design learning-based frameworks for spatial
and temporal super-resolution and denoising of 2D/3D ultrasound images and MRI volumes.
Key contributions include real-time pipelines combining spectral transforms and deep learning,
low-rank denoising methods, and quality-preserving SR of noisy images.

**Multimodal Neuroimaging & AI Diagnosis**: I investigate how AI and SR techniques can support
diagnosis of neurological diseases (Alzheimer's disease, movement disorders) from structural and
functional MRI, PET, and CT data, including quantitative biomarker extraction and harmonisation
across scanners.

**Signal Sampling & Fuzzy Transform**: I develop kernel-based and Fuzzy-transform methods for
optimal sampling, reconstruction, and approximation of arbitrary signals and images, with
applications to biomedical imaging and point-cloud data.

**Graph-based Learning for Clinical Data**: I apply graph neural networks and graph signal
processing to heterogeneous clinical data (EHR, dPET time-activity curves) for drug administration
prediction and simulation of radiotracer concentration in neurological diseases.

**High-Performance Computing**: I design and run large-scale experiments on EuroHPC clusters
(CINECA Marconi100, Leonardo) for training deep learning models and solving sparse linear systems
arising from PDE discretisations.
